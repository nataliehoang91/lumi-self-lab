# UX Copy Reference Guide

## Tone & Voice Principles

**Tone:** Calm, Human, Reflective, Non-Corporate

**Avoid:**
- Performance language ("track performance", "monitor progress")
- Surveillance language ("view activity", "track behavior")
- Managerial pressure ("required", "mandatory", "must")
- Corporate jargon ("leverage", "optimize", "synergy")

**Use:**
- Self-reflection language ("understand yourself", "personal insights")
- Supportive language ("we're here to help", "your journey")
- Empowering language ("you're in control", "your choice")
- Human language ("think of it like", "imagine")

## Help & Guidance Text

### What is an Experiment?
```
An experiment is a structured way to understand yourself better. You define what you want to track, check in regularly, and discover patterns in your own data.

Think of it like a personal research project‚Äîbut just for you.
```

### What are Organizations?
```
Organizations let teams share experiment templates and view aggregate insights. When you link an experiment to an organization, you contribute to collective understanding while keeping your personal reflections private.

You're always in control of what's shared.
```

### Privacy Explained:
```
Privacy is at the heart of Self-Lab. Here's how it works:

Personal Experiments:
‚Ä¢ Completely private to you
‚Ä¢ No one else can see your data
‚Ä¢ You can link to an organization later if you want

Organization-Linked Experiments:
‚Ä¢ Your personal reflections stay private
‚Ä¢ Only aggregate patterns are shared (like average scores)

‚Ä¢ Always private, even if linked to an organization
```

## Accessibility & Clarity

### Always Include:
- Clear action labels
- Privacy indicators (üîí, üè†, üè¢)
- Confirmation for destructive actions
- "Learn More" links for complex concepts
- Escape hatches ("Cancel", "Not now", "Skip")

### Never Use:
- Vague language ("proceed", "continue" without context)
- Assumptions ("as you know", "obviously")
- Pressure language ("don't miss out", "limited time")
- Technical jargon without explanation

## Context-Specific Variations

### When User Has No Orgs:
- Hide org-related options
- Show subtle "Join organization" hints
- Emphasize personal benefits

### When User Has Orgs:
- Show org options but don't push
- Make personal the default
- Explain org benefits clearly

### When Experiment is Org-Linked:
- Always show "Make Personal" option
- Remind about privacy protection
- Show what's shared vs. private

### When Experiment is Personal:
- Show "Link to Organization" option (subtle)
- Emphasize privacy
- No pressure to share
